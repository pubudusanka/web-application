Traveling around Western Province

May 9, 2024 Jennifer Hopps

The Western Province is the bustling heart of Sri Lanka, home to the vibrant capital city of Colombo. This urban hub boasts a blend of modern skyscrapers and colonial-era architecture, with attractions like the iconic Galle Face Green, Pettah Market, and Gangaramaya Temple. Negombo, a coastal town, is renowned for its sandy beaches, colorful fishing boats, and historic Dutch canals. Kalutara offers a tranquil escape with its golden beaches and the iconic Kalutara Bodhiya, a sacred Buddhist temple. Mount Lavinia Beach, just south of Colombo, is perfect for sunbathing and enjoying fresh seafood. For shopaholics and food enthusiasts, Colombo's malls and street markets offer diverse experiences. With its mix of city life, cultural sites, and beach retreats, the Western Province caters to all types of travelers. Read More »

Traveling around Central Province

April 5, 2024 Jennifer Hopps

The Central Province is a paradise for nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts. Kandy, a UNESCO World Heritage City, is home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic and the serene Kandy Lake. The misty hills of Nuwara Eliya, often called "Little England," offer picturesque tea plantations, the breathtaking Hakgala Botanical Garden, and cool weather perfect for relaxing. Ella, a small town surrounded by lush greenery, is famous for its hiking trails, including Little Adam’s Peak and the Nine Arches Bridge. Horton Plains National Park, located near Nuwara Eliya, features the dramatic World’s End cliff and diverse flora and fauna. Tea enthusiasts can visit the numerous estates to witness the tea-making process. The ancient Dambulla Cave Temple and the majestic Sigiriya Rock Fortress, though shared with the North Central Province, are must-visit sites nearby. Central Province is where natural beauty meets cultural heritage. Read More »

Traveling around Southern Province

March 13, 2024 Jennifer Hopps

The Southern Province is a haven for beach lovers and history buffs alike. Galle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, boasts the historic Galle Fort, a 16th-century colonial marvel surrounded by charming streets and boutique shops. Unawatuna and Mirissa are famous for their idyllic beaches, perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and whale watching. Tangalle offers quieter shores and luxury resorts for a more tranquil getaway. Wildlife enthusiasts can visit Yala National Park, home to leopards, elephants, and other exotic animals. Matara's Weherahena Temple and Dondra Lighthouse showcase the region's cultural and historical significance. Surfing enthusiasts flock to Weligama and Hiriketiya for world-class waves. With its stunning coastline, rich history, and diverse activities, the Southern Province is a top destination in Sri Lanka. Read More »

Traveling around Nothern Province

February 12, 2024 Jennifer Hopps

The Northern Province, known for its unique Tamil culture, offers a distinct travel experience. Jaffna, the region’s main city, is a cultural hub with attractions like the Nallur Kandaswamy Temple and the Jaffna Fort. The unspoiled islands of Delft and Nagadeepa are ideal for exploring history and spirituality. Kayts and Karainagar islands feature pristine beaches and traditional fishing villages. The Keerimalai Springs and Casuarina Beach provide serene spots to relax. Visitors can learn about the region’s history at the Jaffna Library and the remnants of colonial Dutch and Portuguese buildings. The Northern Province also offers authentic Tamil cuisine, including flavorful curries and traditional sweets. It’s a destination for those seeking off-the-beaten-path adventures.Read More »

Traveling around Eastern Province

December 20, 2023 Jennifer Hopps

The Eastern Province is known for its untouched beaches and rich cultural heritage. Trincomalee, with its turquoise waters, is perfect for snorkeling, diving, and whale watching at Pigeon Island. The Koneswaram Temple, perched on Swami Rock, offers breathtaking views and spiritual solace. Arugam Bay, a world-famous surfing destination, attracts adventure seekers from around the globe. Batticaloa Lagoon and its historic lighthouse provide picturesque settings for relaxation. Pasikudah and Kalkudah beaches are ideal for calm waters and luxury resorts. The region is also rich in history, with ancient ruins and temples like the Seruwila Mangala Raja Maha Viharaya. The Eastern Province combines adventure, tranquility, and history in one package.Read More »

Traveling around North Central Province

September 1, 2023 Jennifer Hopps

The North Central Province is the cradle of Sri Lanka’s ancient civilization, offering an array of UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Anuradhapura, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, features sacred stupas like Ruwanwelisaya and the Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi tree. Polonnaruwa, another ancient capital, is home to stunning ruins, including the Gal Vihara rock carvings. Sigiriya, the iconic rock fortress, offers panoramic views and fascinating frescoes. Minneriya National Park provides a chance to witness the famous "Gathering" of elephants. Mihintale, considered the birthplace of Buddhism in Sri Lanka, is a sacred pilgrimage site. The Ritigala Forest Monastery offers a tranquil escape into nature and history. This province is a treasure trove of archaeological and natural wonders.Read More »

Traveling around North Western Province

July 27, 2023 Jennifer Hopps

The North Western Province is a region of cultural and ecological significance. Wilpattu National Park, Sri Lanka’s largest wildlife sanctuary, is a haven for safari enthusiasts, with leopards, sloth bears, and a variety of bird species. Yapahuwa, an ancient rock fortress, offers historical intrigue and stunning views. Panduwasnuwara, the ancient capital, showcases ruins of palaces and temples. Chilaw and Puttalam feature scenic lagoons, mangroves, and traditional fishing villages. The Munneswaram Temple, a revered Hindu site, attracts pilgrims year-round. Kalpitiya is an emerging destination for kite surfing and dolphin watching. The North Western Province blends history, spirituality, and outdoor adventures. Read More »

Traveling around Sabaragamuwa Province

July 27, 2023 Jennifer Hopps

The Sabaragamuwa Province is rich in natural beauty, featuring waterfalls, forests, and cultural landmarks. Sinharaja Forest Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a biodiversity hotspot perfect for trekking and bird watching. Bopath Ella and Bambarakanda Falls are stunning waterfalls that draw nature enthusiasts. Ratnapura, the "City of Gems," is the center of Sri Lanka's gem trade and offers a chance to visit gem mines and museums. Udawalawe National Park is ideal for spotting elephants in their natural habitat. The Sabaragamuwa Maha Saman Devalaya is a key religious site. Adventure seekers can explore the Kitulgala rainforest for white-water rafting. This province offers a mix of adventure, nature, and culture. Read More »

Traveling around Uva Province

June 14, 2023 Jennifer Hopps

The Uva Province is a picturesque region known for its breathtaking landscapes and serene atmosphere. Bandarawela and Badulla are charming towns surrounded by tea plantations and waterfalls. Horton Plains National Park, shared with the Central Province, offers dramatic views at World’s End and scenic hikes. Ella is a favorite for travelers seeking natural beauty, with attractions like the Demodara Nine Arches Bridge and Ella Rock. Lipton’s Seat provides panoramic views of tea estates. Diyaluma Falls, Sri Lanka’s second-highest waterfall, is a must-visit. The Muthiyangana Temple in Badulla adds cultural depth to the province. Uva is a perfect retreat for relaxation and exploration.
